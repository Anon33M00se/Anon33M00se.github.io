How does this site work?

A few people have asked me about how I manage this site. It changes frequently, as I will often throw stuff up very quickly, move it around, and take it down again.

The whole site is managed by editing local files in emacs and then a couple of quick keystrokes push out any changes to free webhosting on github. I'll explain each of the pieces in turn.

It Starts with Org Mode

Org mode is a part of Emacs, and is a whole ecosystem for, well, organizing information. Org files are just plain text with some formatting conventions not unlike Markdown. Here's an example:

That is the exact org content that this page starts with. Because I spend most of my day in Emacs already (see email), it is super easy to just pop into an org file, make some changes (or open a new file) and automatically push the changes out to "production".

You can do a lot of different kinds of formats in org mode very easily. For example: 

*bold*, _underline_, +strikethrough+, /italics/

| n | n^2 | n^3 |
|---+-----+-----|
| 1 |   1 |   1 |
| 2 |   4 |   8 |
| 3 |   9 |  27 |

$y=x^2$

leads to:

bold, underline, strikethrough, italics

n n2 n3
1 1 1
2 4 8
3 9 27

\(y=x^2\)

But this is just scratching the surface or org mode. To quote from the Org FAQ:

In its default setup, Org-mode offers a simple outlining and task management environment. But it also has a huge number of features and settings "under the hood." Advanced users have configured Org-mode for almost every conceivable use scenario.
